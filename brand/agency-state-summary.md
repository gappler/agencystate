# Agency State — Strategic Foundation

**URL:** agencystate.ai
**Tagline:** AI for business and marketing

---

## Brand Identity

**Name rationale:** "Agency" carries a double meaning — the agency world the founding team comes from, and agency as the ability to act independently. "State" is the condition you achieve. Agency State is the point where your team stops waiting on vendors, IT, and engineering and starts acting on its own.

**Visual identity:** Wordmark in Space Grotesk — "agency" bold (700), "state" regular (400), set as a single string. Monogram icon: rounded square with lowercase "a" (bold) and "s" (regular). The weight contrast is the identity. See `brand/agency-state-brand-guidelines.md` for full specifications.

**Tone:** Professional, authoritative, cutting edge. The brand should demonstrate marketing taste and strategic awareness.

**Core metaphor:** Claude Code as a partner — a multiplier of you. Not about building software. About amplifying what one person or one team can do. Efficiency, optimization, transformation, independence.

---

## Positioning

**One-liner:** Agency State teaches business teams to work with AI as a desktop partner — using Claude Code and related tools to amplify their capability, build what they need, and stop depending on off-the-shelf software that's 70% right.

**Target persona:** The 5–10% of business professionals who are already power users of AI, technically curious, not afraid of getting hands dirty. They're frustrated by tool limitations and ready to move from using AI to working *with* AI. They're at companies doing $3M–$100M in revenue with teams of 2–10 people.

**What Agency State is not:** A creative agency. A general AI consultancy. A vendor trainer. A vibe coding bootcamp.

---

## Positioning Architecture: Hub and Spoke

**Hub (brand level):** "We teach business teams to amplify their capability with Claude Code."

**First spoke (launch):** Marketing teams. Agency State's founding team has deep credibility here — decades of agency experience, brand-side roles, and internal agency leadership. Zero competitors at the intersection of Claude Code + marketing teams + paid training.

**Second spoke (months 3–6):** Operations and sales ops. Largest non-engineer builder population. Strongest build-vs-buy pain (35% workflow automation replacement pressure per Retool 2026 data).

**Third spoke (months 6–12):** Finance. Highest budget authority, strongest ROI math, but requires governance and compliance curriculum.

**Expansion signal:** Don't launch new spokes proactively. Wait for early cohort participants to say "can you do this for our ops team?"

## Founding DNA and Talent Model

Agency State is built by and for agency professionals — people who've spent careers building marketing and business systems for complex organizations across verticals. The founding expertise spans digital agencies (Magnet Interactive, Grafik), brand-side operations (Lincoln Mercury, Bacardi Global, Nissan/Infiniti), and internal agency leadership at scale (AIA). The common thread isn't a vertical — it's the agency mindset: diagnose the problem, build the system, deliver the outcome.

**Talent pool:** Future hires and collaborators should come from the agency world — professionals who understand client service, can translate business problems into technical solutions, and are comfortable working across industries. Agency veterans bring the right combination of strategic thinking, executional speed, and client empathy. They also intuitively understand the value of building custom solutions because they've spent careers doing it.

**Association expertise** is part of the team's background and a valuable community connection, but not a brand anchor. Leading with associations risks signaling "small budgets, slow-moving" to higher-value targets.

## Target Markets

### By vertical (ranked for capability-building training)

1. **E-commerce/DTC ($3M–$50M revenue)** — Fast decisions, high SaaS spend, technically adventurous teams, clear ROI
1b. **Digital agencies** — Multiple functions suffering SaaS overload, multiplier effect (agency builds for clients), most technically curious non-developer staff
2. **SaaS companies (Series A–B)** — Already active builders, resource-constrained, culture values building
3. **Professional services** — Budget exists, entry point is marketing, expand to ops and client delivery
4. **Associations** — Lowest budget, best served with lower-priced async content or community. Positioning play, not revenue driver

### By company size

**Sweet spot: 25–200 employees.**
- Below 25: training founders/solopreneurs, not teams
- Above 200: enterprise procurement, IT governance, internal engineering expectations
- 25–200: teams wear multiple hats, no internal tools team, budget for training ($5K–$15K) but not custom dev ($50K–$200K), decision-maker can say yes without procurement

## Product Roadmap

### Immediate (months 1–6)
- **"Build Your Own MarTech" cohort** — 4-week live program, $1,500–$2,000/person, 10–15 participants. Week 1: from prompting to building (Lovable on-ramp, then Claude Code fundamentals). Weeks 2–3: build specific tools (reporting dashboard, competitive intelligence, content workflow). Week 4: participant-specific builds. Every participant leaves with 3–4 working tools.
- **Productized AI Readiness Assessment** — $3,500–$7,500, fixed scope. Same framework across verticals, customized output.

### Growth (months 3–12)
- **"Build Your Own Ops Stack" cohort** — same format, adapted for operations/sales ops teams
- **Skills library subscription** — $50–$150/month, curated marketing-specific Claude Code skills

### Scale (months 6–18)
- **Company-level engagement** — train 2–3 functions at one company, $8K–$15K
- **Micro-SaaS tools** — built from patterns discovered in training delivery, $2K–$8K MRR each
- **Corporate workshops** — $5K–$15K per engagement for larger teams

### Revenue trajectory
- Year 1 (conservative): $150K–$300K from cohorts and assessments
- Year 2: $500K–$800K adding company-level engagements and subscriptions
- Year 3 target: 60% products and training, 40% consulting — the inversion point

## Competitive Landscape

**Zero direct competitors** at the intersection of Claude Code + business teams + paid training with build-vs-buy ROI framing.

**Adjacent competitors:**
- **Paul Roetzer / Marketing AI Institute** — dominates broad AI marketing education, media company model, enterprise focus, $75K+ workshops. Doesn't serve small teams, doesn't teach building.
- **Dan Sanchez** — solo consultant, transparent pricing ($250/hr, $5K/mo retainer), generalist marketing AI.
- **Harold Dijkstra (Maven)** — Prototyping with AI Bootcamp, $799, 1,500+ students. Targets PMs, not marketers or business teams. Tool-agnostic, not Claude-specific.
- **Liam Ottley / AAA Accelerator** — teaches AI automation agency building, 3,800+ students. Targets aspiring agency owners, not in-house teams.
- **CCforEveryone.com** — free Claude Code course for non-engineers. No business model, no cohort, no marketing focus. Proves demand.
- **Anthropic Academy** — 13 free courses, developer/architect focused. Nothing for business professionals building internal tools.

**Key differentiator:** Not "learn Claude Code." It's "amplify your team's capability — leave with working tools your team uses Monday morning."
