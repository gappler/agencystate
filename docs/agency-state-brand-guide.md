# Agency State — Brand Guide

The single working resource for writing Agency State copy. When copy decisions are unclear, this is the tiebreaker. Update this doc when something changes — don't let drift go unresolved.

---

## 1. The offer

Agency State teaches business and marketing professionals how to produce the strategic, operational, or deliverable outputs their business and clients demand — using Claude and Claude Code.

## 2. Audience

**Primary (beachhead):** Agency people — account leads, strategists, creatives, and producers in internal and external agencies serving mid-to-large B2C and B2B clients. The muscle they already have — learning a client's business fast — is the exact muscle this methodology extends.

**Adjacent:** Business and marketing teams in mid-sized companies.

**Within the audience, we serve the innovators:** professionals who care about their work and themselves, who want to stay sharp, and who want to stand out.

## 3. The tension

They got into the work to do strategy. The work keeps pulling them into output. Most AI adoption makes that worse — faster tactics, same trap. Agency State addresses the strategic layer, not the tactical one.

## 4. The method

**Context and conversation.** The practice has two core skills, and they work together to produce real outputs.

- **Context** is what Claude needs to do meaningful work on your behalf — your brand, your audience, your workflows, your judgment about what good looks like. You build it as a knowledge base, so every output is customized to your business.
- **Conversation** is how you direct the work, draw out Claude's thinking, and shape the output into something useful.

Context without conversation produces a well-briefed partner with nothing to aim at. Conversation without context produces articulate output that misses your reality. Together they produce work that actually serves your business.

**Four steps in the cohort:**

1. Build your knowledge base
2. Build the outputs your work demands
3. Build content that sounds like you
4. Show what you built (demo day)

## 5. What makes us different

Most AI courses teach the tool. Agency State teaches the method — context and conversation — that works across any business problem. The evidence is the outputs. The method is the differentiator.

## 7. Voice

- **Dry, direct, lightly self-aware.** Trust the work to impress.
- **Plain over jargon.** If a marketing exec wouldn't use the word in conversation, rewrite.
- **Concrete over abstract.** Name the output, the timeframe, the number.
- **Confident without overclaiming.** "It works" beats "it transforms."
- **Warm without being soft.** Honest about limits. No hedging.

## 8. Never say

- **Fear framing** — "don't get left behind," "AI is coming for your job," "your competitors are already doing this."
- **Overclaim** — "transform your career," "change your life," "10x your output," "save 20 hours a week."
- **SaaS clichés** — "leverage," "unlock," "amplify," "empower," "disrupt," "synergy," "game-changer," "revolutionize."
- **Consultant-speak** — "our proprietary methodology," "best-in-class," "thought leadership."
- **Fake proof** — testimonial-style claims or outcome numbers before the first cohort finishes.
- **Hedge words** — "we believe," "we think," "may," "might."
- **"Tools" as a rhetorical category.** Use the word concretely (a specific tool, a tool count). Never as shorthand for what we teach — we teach a method that produces outputs, not tools.

## 9. Words with specific meaning

- **Outputs** — what professionals produce. Covers the full range: strategic, operational, deliverable. Load-bearing word.
- **Context** — what the knowledge base provides. What makes outputs yours, not generic.
- **Conversation** — the skill of directing Claude's work.
- **Method** — what Agency State teaches. Not a course, not a tool, not a product.
- **Produce** — the verb for what professionals do. Active, concrete, ownable.
- **Build** — the verb for how professionals work with Claude. Also active, concrete.
- **Professionals** — default audience noun in public copy.
- **Agency people** — used when the beachhead is specifically invoked.
- **Knowledge base** — the thing students build. Concrete. Used in curriculum language.

## Open tensions (resolve before the next major copy pass)

Flagged so they don't drift silently.

- **"Scale yourself" in the hero.** On the never-say list (SaaS cliché; treats the human as an asset to be multiplied). Currently working because it's the punchline of a three-beat rhythm and literally describes what the program delivers. Pressure-test against: "Learn Claude Code. Build real outputs. Work like a team of one." If the original still feels stronger after trying the alternative, keep it and let this exception stand.

- **Tool-forward framing on the site.** The hero and body copy lead with "build working tools," which contradicts section 4, 5, and 8 of this guide. Site needs to evolve toward outputs-first language.

- **Pricing and curriculum format.** Research (in `research/`) says $500 is bottom-of-market for this curriculum and that 8 hours of live instruction over 4 weeks is heavier than competitors at this price. Not a brand-guide decision, but affects how aggressively you can claim value in copy. Current pricing may need revisiting before Cohort 2.
