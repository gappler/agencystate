# Agency State: Curriculum Pressure-Test (Revised)

My apologies for the initial miscategorization. I have re-read your curriculum through the correct lens: **professional application of Claude Code for business and marketing workflows, not app building.** 

I have benchmarked your curriculum against programs that actually compete in this specific space, such as Recalc Academy's "Claude Code for Business Users" [1], Growclass's "AI Marketing & Strategy Certification" [2], and Section's "AI for Strategic Decision Making" [3].

Here are the revised, direct answers to your six questions based on this correct framing.

## 1. Content Alignment: What's Missing and What's Extra?

Your curriculum is exceptionally strong in teaching the *methodology* of AI-assisted work (Understand → Build → Reflect). The progression from static data (Session 1) to planning (Session 2) to content generation (Session 3) is logically sound for a marketing professional.

**What buyers expect that you are missing:**
*   **Email and Calendar Management:** Recalc Academy explicitly leads with "triage your inbox, manage your to-do lists, share availability" [1]. For a business user, the inbox is the biggest pain point. Showing how Claude Code can automate email drafting or calendar parsing is a massive quick win that your curriculum currently skips.
*   **Document and Data Room Review:** Recalc also highlights using Claude Code to "summarize, categorize, and flag across many files" [1]. Marketers deal with massive strategy decks, competitor research, and brand guidelines. Teaching them how to use Claude Code to instantly synthesize a folder of PDFs is highly valuable.
*   **The "Personal Knowledge Base":** Teaching users how to make Claude Code remember their preferences over time (using memory or persistent files) is a key selling point for business users [1].

**What you are including that nobody else is:**
*   **The "Foundation First" Brand Definition:** Your requirement to build a brand/business definition and generate synthetic data *before* building tools is unique and highly strategic. It forces marketers to think about *context* before *execution*. This is a major differentiator from courses that just teach generic prompt templates.

## 2. Session Density: How Your 8 Hours Compare

You are offering 4 live sessions (2 hours each) for a total of 8 hours, plus required homework.

Here is the actual live hour breakdown of your competitors in the business/strategy space:
*   **Recalc Academy ($795):** 3 hours live (two 1.5-hour sessions) [1].
*   **Section ($195 or membership):** 1.5 hours live (single workshop) [3].
*   **Growclass ($1,999):** 6 weeks, estimated 3-5 hours per week (mix of live and async) [2].

**The Verdict:** At 8 hours of mandatory live instruction, you are still asking for significantly more live time than direct competitors like Recalc Academy. 

Two hours is a long time for a live session. Recalc's 90-minute format is much more digestible for busy professionals. If you keep the 2-hour format, you must ensure the "Build" and "Reflect" portions are highly interactive to prevent fatigue.

## 3. The Prework Gamble

Your prework requires installing Claude Code, creating a project folder, writing a `CLAUDE.md` file, building a business definition, and generating synthetic data.

**This remains a massive gamble.** 

Even for business users, the terminal is intimidating. Recalc Academy handles this by sending "Installation instructions sent in advance so you're ready on day one *if you'd like to experiment alongside the course*" [1]. They make it optional for the first session, focusing on demonstration first.

By requiring terminal setup *before* the first session, you risk losing participants who get stuck on `npm install` and feel too embarrassed to attend Session 1.

**Recommendation:** Keep the business definition and synthetic data generation as prework (using the standard Claude web interface). Move the actual Claude Code terminal installation to the first 30 minutes of Session 1, where you can guide them through it live and troubleshoot immediately.

## 4. Session 4 Risk: The "Bring Your Own Data" Problem

Asking non-technical marketers to bring their own messy, unstructured company data and build a tool live in 40 minutes is still the highest risk point in the curriculum.

Real CRM exports and analytics dumps are notoriously messy. Claude Code will spend 30 minutes just trying to parse the CSV formatting, leaving no time for actual tool building. Participants will get frustrated and leave the final session feeling defeated.

**Recommendation:** Change Session 4. Instead of a live independent build with real data, have participants build their custom tool as the *homework* between Session 3 and 4. Use Session 4 as a "Demo Day" where everyone presents what they built, and you do live code reviews and strategic teardowns. This ensures they have time to wrestle with the messy data asynchronously, and the live session focuses on celebration and refinement.

## 5. What Would I Cut? (Trimming to 3 Sessions)

If you had to trim this to three sessions to match the market standard for a $500-$800 course (closer to Recalc's 3 hours), here is what you cut:

**Merge Session 2 (Planning) and Session 3 (Content).**

*   Session 1 (Dashboards/Reports) proves the concept and gets them a quick win.
*   Session 2 (New merged session) covers both planning models and content generation. You can teach the *concept* of sequential building without needing a dedicated 2-hour session just for planning tools.
*   Session 3 (Demo Day/Real Data) becomes the capstone.

This compresses the timeline, maintains momentum, and reduces the cognitive load, bringing your course closer to the 4.5–6 hour sweet spot.

## 6. Pricing Fit: $500 vs. $1,200

**At $500:** You are massively over-delivering. Recalc Academy charges $795 for just 3 hours of instruction [1]. Your 8 hours of highly structured, strategic instruction is a steal at $500.

**At $1,200:** This curriculum absolutely justifies $1,200+ for Cohort 2, *provided you are targeting the right buyer*. 

To confidently charge $1,200+, you are competing with programs like Growclass ($1,999) [2]. These buyers are paying for **business transformation**, not just tool training. Your focus on "Foundation First" and strategic alignment perfectly positions you for this higher price point. 

To solidify the $1,200 price, ensure your marketing emphasizes the *business outcomes* (e.g., "Automate your weekly reporting," "Generate strategic briefs in minutes") rather than the technical features of Claude Code.
